Week 1 Notes/Exercises

    How To Rewire Your Brain for Mobile First UX

  • Designing for the smallest device first will help lead to the development of simple, beautiful apps.
  • Developers spent nearly 30 whole years designing for a single device.
  • In 2007, the iPhone released, and this caused a huge decline in desktop usage.
  • Now, instead of there being one device in a household, there are now 2 or 3 per person.
  • Full mobile-first rewrites are time consuming and expensive.
  • Start small and scale up.

    How to Improve UX by Understanding Users

  • Be There. You have to make sure that you are available to your customers with a mobile version of your site.
  • Be Useful. Do not make your users hunt for content.
  • Be Quick. Make processes simple and make the interactions with the site smooth and seamless.
  • 75% of people rely on their thumbs when using a mobile device.
  • We need to design and develop apps according to how the general population interact with their devices.
  • Reduce images on the site if possible.
  • Optimize and minify CSS or JS files.

    Better Mobile UX with Strategic Menu Design

  • We need to care about how users interact with the software that we create.
  • Main nav bars are easier to access at the bottom of the screen.
  • Moving primary controls helped Facebook create the illusion tha their site is faster.
  • Buttons need to be wrapped in a border so that people know that the word Menu can be interacted with.
